Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Photo by That's Her Business on Unsplash

A discussion about the 10 principals of Neuroplasticity, and a realisation that I need to develop a Growth Mindset moving forward.

Neuroplasticity

The 10 Principals of Neuroplasticity

  • Use it or lose it
  • Use it and improve it
  • Specificity
  • Repetition matters
  • Intensity Matters
  • Time Matters
  • Salience Matters
  • Age Matters
  • Transference
  • Interferance

Did you ever go to the beach, play in the sand, and build a sandcastle, that had a few trenches for the water to flow around? Then you filled your bucket up full of sea water and ran back to your castle, and poured the water in the trenches. The feeling of satisfaction and joy when you witness your beautiful sandcastle, complete with a flowing water feature?

Neuroplasticity works in a similar way. The trenches are our neural pathways, and the water is the knowledge. More water = strenghtened pathways and retained knowledge. Without the water, the trenches dry up.

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

If your intention is to learn, or get better at a certain skill, such as learning an instrument, then having an understanding of the principals of neuroplasticity can definitely benefit the way you structure your approach to learning.

At the beginning of the learning journey, you will pick up skills fast. You then need to repeat what you have learned in order to retain that information. There will be days where things get difficult, so focusing on areas where intensity can lead to deeper understanding is necessary. Over time, through consistent repetition, you will get better at your skill, and those neural pathways will have strengthened. Without that consistent practice over time, you will lose that skill or knowledge.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

At the moment, I am learning Korean, so that I can watch K-Dramas and variety shows without English subtitles. I set aside 1 hour, usually before I sleep, to use a language learning app to study phrases, pronunciation and Hangul.

Because of my need to watch K-dramas, without subtitles, I now have a specific reason to build, and strengthen those neural pathways, otherwise I’ll be watching subtitles for the rest of my life. To learn new phrases, you need practice and patience. Repetition is needed in order to not forget what you just learned. You need a challenge, like having a simple conversation with someone, in order to build intensity with the learning. Over time, I hope to be fluent enough to have conversations over K-BBQ and soju… The skill to learn one language can transfer into learning another language.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

I think, more than anything, you need to have a goal, a strong reason why you need to learn that skill. This will your driving force that will pull you through when it gets tough.

Look for different types of experiences that are related to your goal. For example, in my journey of learning Korean, I could look for native speakers to practice with, watch short clips without subtitles and so on. The engagement itself is rewarding, and continuous repetition will make those pathways more permanent.

Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

The Growth mindset simply refers to the belief that skills and intelligence can be improved upon through effort and persistence. With the growth mindset, there is no limit to your ability to grow and learn, even in failure.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

Doing this exploration on both neuroplasticity and developing a growth mindset helped me assess different areas of my life. I could see where I believed that I had a fixed mindset, and other areas where I believe I had a growth mindset.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I think for me, realizing that there is no need to put a cap on your learning ability, especially when it comes to areas where I feel a little out of my depth. Knowing what you know, and knowing what you don’t know, will greatly narrow down the learning scope. Before entering Dev Academy, the thought of JavaScript programming really terrified me. I think the time factor is what caused me to put a fixed mindset on my ability to learn. The thought that I had to quickly learn these concepts in order to carry out the tasks. I now know that learning is a process, not a race.

Reflect and discuss how these understandings may shape or influence your learning plan and strategy.

I had already heard of the typical statements like “use it or lose it” and “repetition makes perfect” before, but I was never really aware of where they stem from. Learning about neuroplasticity has greatly affected how I will plan for future goals that I want to achieve and skills that I want to improve upon. Repetition, intensity will be the principals I will focus on incorporating into my learning plan.
