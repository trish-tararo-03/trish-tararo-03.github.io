Emotional Intelligence

A discussion on what it is, and the important role it plays in our everyday lives.

What is Emotional Intelligence?

" To recognize, understand and manage our own emotions. To recognize, understand and influence the emotions of others.
Being aware that emotions can drive out behavior and impact people (positively and negatively) and learning how to manage those emotions - both our own and others - especially when we are under pressure. - Daniel Goleman

Emotional intelligence is the ability to recognize, perceive, and evaluate your emotions, and the emotions of others. It is the ability to be conscious of our emotions and how they can influence our everyday lives. One who has low emotional intelligence may let their emotions overtake their rational thinking, which could ultimately lead to negative results. One who had high emotional intelligence is able to understand their emotions, and the emotions of others, and use that knowledge to influence their rational thinking or decision making.

How is it different to IQ?

Your IQ refers to your intellectual ability - the ability to use logic to solve problems, to understand abstract ideas, to plan and strategize, to use language to convey ideas. If emotional intelligence is related to behavior, then intellectual ability relates to the information we use to make decisions. Everyone has an IQ

Why is it important?

If everyone made decisions based on facts and science, we would definitely get the same sets of answers or results. Science relies on what is and what isnâ€™t. The Boolean truth! Emotions add color. Emotions add another path, another layer when making decisions. Emotions is the brightly colored gray area between what we believe to be true and what the facts state is true. It is the reason we can leave everything we know behind for one person, or do the opposite, and hide ourselves away from the world. When the facts are placed in-front of us, it is our emotions that drive the final decision making process. The majority of the human race makes decisions on the way we feel. We feel this is the best choice because of X, Y, Z. We believe we are making the right choice based on X, Y, Z.

If we can master our emotions, we can gain control over them, instead of our emotions controlling us. By improving our emotional intelligence, we can improve our understanding, and improve our power to influence others.
